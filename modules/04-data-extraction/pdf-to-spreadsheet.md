## PDF to Spreadsheet

**Instructor:** Jabbir Basha, Principal AI Engineer

---

### Why Businesses Need PDF Data Extraction

Every day, businesses receive critical information trapped inside PDF files — vendor invoices, purchase orders, bank statements, contracts, and regulatory filings. Manually retyping this data into spreadsheets wastes hours and introduces errors. A single misplaced decimal can cascade into a budgeting disaster.

Claude Cowork can read PDFs, understand their structure, and output the data in a spreadsheet-ready format — all from a single prompt.

---

### Setting Up Your PDF Processing Folder

Before you start, organize your files so Cowork can work efficiently.

1. Create a dedicated folder on your computer (for example, `Invoices` on your Desktop).
2. Place all the PDFs you want to process inside that folder.
3. Keep file names descriptive when possible (for example, `Acme-Corp-Jan-2026.pdf` rather than `scan001.pdf`).

A clean folder structure helps Cowork process files faster and reduces the chance of missed documents.

---

### Example Prompts

Here are prompts you can use or adapt for your own needs.

**Basic extraction:**

> Read all PDFs in my Invoices folder and create an Excel spreadsheet with vendor name, date, amount, and category.

**With additional detail:**

> Go through every PDF in my Invoices folder. For each one, extract the vendor name, invoice number, date, line items, subtotal, tax, and total. Put everything into a single spreadsheet with one row per invoice.

**Targeting a specific file:**

> Open the PDF called "Acme-Corp-Jan-2026.pdf" and pull out all the line items into a spreadsheet. Include the item description, quantity, unit price, and total for each line.

### How Cowork Reads and Interprets PDF Content

When you point Cowork at a PDF, here is what happens behind the scenes:

1. **Document scanning** — Cowork opens the file and reads through every page.
2. **Structure recognition** — It identifies tables, headings, paragraphs, and key-value pairs (like "Invoice Date: 01/15/2026").
3. **Data extraction** — It pulls the relevant fields based on your prompt.
4. **Organization** — It maps the extracted data into rows and columns that make sense for a spreadsheet.
5. **Output** — It writes the result to your chosen format.

You do not need to tell Cowork where on the page to look. It figures out the layout on its own.

---

### Handling Different PDF Formats

Not all PDFs are created equal. Here is how Cowork handles the two main types.

#### Digital PDFs (text-based)

These are PDFs created directly from software — think invoices generated by accounting tools or reports exported from dashboards. The text is embedded in the file and easy to read. Cowork handles these with high accuracy.

#### Scanned PDFs (image-based)

These are PDFs created by scanning a paper document. The content is essentially a photograph. Cowork uses optical character recognition (OCR) capabilities to read the text from the image. Accuracy depends on scan quality:

- **High-resolution, clean scans** — Excellent results.
- **Low-resolution or skewed scans** — Cowork will do its best, but you may need to verify a few fields.

**Tip:** If you have a choice, always prefer digital PDFs. If you must scan, use at least 300 DPI and make sure the document is flat and well-lit.

### Output Formats

Cowork can produce your extracted data in several formats:

| Format | Best For |
|--------|----------|
| **CSV** | Universal compatibility. Opens in Excel, Google Sheets, Numbers, and most data tools. |
| **Excel (.xlsx)** | When you need formatting, multiple sheets, or formulas. |
| **Google Sheets compatible** | When you want to upload directly to Google Drive for sharing and collaboration. |

To request a specific format, simply mention it in your prompt:

> Extract the data and save it as a CSV file.

> Create an Excel spreadsheet with the results.

If you do not specify, Cowork will default to the format that best fits your request.

### Step-by-Step Walkthrough

Let us walk through a complete example from start to finish.

#### Before: What You Start With

A folder called `Invoices` containing three PDF files:

- `Acme-Corp-Jan-2026.pdf` — A digital invoice for office supplies ($1,245.00)
- `GlobalTech-Feb-2026.pdf` — A digital invoice for software licenses ($3,500.00)
- `QuickPrint-Jan-2026.pdf` — A scanned invoice for printing services ($287.50)

#### Step 1 — Write Your Prompt

> Read all the PDFs in my Invoices folder and create an Excel spreadsheet. Include columns for vendor name, invoice date, invoice number, description, and total amount. Sort the results by date.

#### Step 2 — Let Cowork Process

Cowork opens each PDF, identifies the relevant fields, and compiles the results. This typically takes a few seconds per file.

#### Step 3 — Review the Output

Cowork produces a file called something like `Invoice_Summary.xlsx` with the following content:

| Vendor Name | Invoice Date | Invoice Number | Description | Total Amount |
|-------------|-------------|----------------|-------------|-------------|
| Acme Corp | 2026-01-12 | INV-4521 | Office supplies | $1,245.00 |
| QuickPrint | 2026-01-28 | QP-0892 | Printing services | $287.50 |
| GlobalTech | 2026-02-03 | GT-2026-118 | Software licenses | $3,500.00 |

#### After: What You End Up With

A single, clean spreadsheet with all your invoice data in one place — sorted, structured, and ready for analysis or import into your accounting software.

### Tips for Best Results

- **Be specific in your prompt.** Name the exact columns you want rather than saying "extract everything."
- **Use consistent file naming.** It helps Cowork (and you) keep track of what has been processed.
- **Verify scanned documents.** Spot-check a few rows when working with scanned PDFs to make sure OCR was accurate.
- **Process in batches.** If you have hundreds of PDFs, break them into smaller batches (20-30 at a time) for smoother processing.
