## Introduction

## Setting Up a Competitive Analysis Project

A competitive analysis project in Cowork is simply a structured series of research prompts focused on your competitors. Here is how to set one up:

1. **Define your competitive landscape.** Identify the industry, market segment, and the competitors you want to track.
2. **Choose your comparison criteria.** Decide what matters most — pricing, features, customer reviews, market share, geographic reach, or something else.
3. **Create a dedicated folder.** Keep all analysis outputs in one place for easy reference and future updates.
4. **Write your initial research prompt.** Be specific about what you need.

### Example Prompt

> "Find pricing information for all major project management tools — including Asana, Monday.com, Trello, ClickUp, Notion, Basecamp, Wrike, Smartsheet, Jira, and Teamwork — and compare their features. Include pricing tiers, free plan availability, key differentiators, and user ratings. Output the results as a spreadsheet."

## Tracking Competitor Changes Over Time

One-time research is useful, but ongoing tracking provides much deeper insight. You can ask Cowork to revisit competitors periodically and flag what has changed.

### Example Prompts for Ongoing Tracking

> "Compare the current pricing for Asana, Monday.com, and ClickUp against the pricing data in my competitor-pricing.xlsx file. Highlight any changes."

> "Check the websites of our top five competitors and summarize any new product announcements or feature releases from the past 30 days."

By keeping previous research outputs in your Cowork workspace, you give Cowork the context it needs to identify differences and trends across time.

## Market Trend Analysis

Beyond individual competitors, Cowork can help you understand broader market dynamics.

### Example Prompts

> "Research the top five trends shaping the project management software market in 2026. Include data points and sources."

> "What are analysts saying about the future of remote work tools? Summarize key predictions and cite your sources."

> "Identify emerging startups in the AI-powered productivity space that have raised funding in the last 12 months."

These prompts help you see beyond your direct competitors and understand the forces that are reshaping your industry.

## Creating SWOT Analysis Reports

A SWOT analysis (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats) is a classic strategic framework. Cowork can generate one based on its web research.

### Example Prompt

> "Based on your research, create a SWOT analysis for our company compared to Asana, Monday.com, and ClickUp. Format it as a four-quadrant table with bullet points in each section."

Cowork will draw on the competitive data it has gathered and organize it into the SWOT framework. You can then refine the output by asking follow-up questions or adding your own internal knowledge.

## Real Estate Market Research Example

Competitive analysis is not limited to software. One of the use cases highlighted in this course is real estate market research.

### Example Prompts

> "Research the average home prices in the top 10 neighborhoods in Austin, Texas. Include year-over-year price changes, average days on market, and inventory levels. Output the results as a table."

> "Compare commercial rental rates for office space in downtown Denver versus the suburban tech corridor. Include average price per square foot, vacancy rates, and lease terms."

> "Summarize the key factors driving residential real estate demand in the Southeast United States in 2026."

These prompts demonstrate how Cowork can gather, compare, and summarize market data across any industry — not just technology.

## Outputting Results to Spreadsheets and Reports

Cowork can format its findings in a way that is ready for immediate use:

- **Spreadsheets** — Ask Cowork to create a CSV or Excel-compatible table with specific columns.
- **Written reports** — Request a narrative summary with headers, bullet points, and key takeaways.
- **Presentation-ready slides** — Ask for content organized by slide, with titles and bullet points.

### Example Prompt

> "Take your competitor analysis and output it as a CSV file with the following columns: Company Name, Free Plan, Starter Price, Pro Price, Enterprise Price, Key Features, User Rating, and Notes."
