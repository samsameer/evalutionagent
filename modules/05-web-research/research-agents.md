## Introduction

Claude Cowork can go beyond your local files and tap into the open web to gather information, compare data, and compile findings. In this lesson, you will learn how Cowork's web browsing capability works, how to enable it, and how to write prompts that produce high-quality research results.

---

## How Cowork's Web Browsing Capability Works

When you assign a research task, Cowork follows a structured process behind the scenes:

1. **Search** — Cowork queries the web using the keywords and intent from your prompt.
2. **Visit pages** — It opens relevant results and reads through the content on each page.
3. **Extract information** — Key data points, facts, and figures are pulled from the pages it visits.
4. **Compile** — Everything is organized into a coherent response or report based on your instructions.

This means you do not need to visit dozens of websites yourself. Cowork handles the browsing, reading, and note-taking, then delivers a clean summary.

---

## Enabling Web Access in Settings

Before Cowork can browse the web, you need to make sure web access is turned on:

1. Open your Cowork workspace.
2. Navigate to **Settings** > **Agent Capabilities**.
3. Find the **Web Browsing** toggle and switch it to **Enabled**.
4. (Optional) Configure any domain restrictions or safe browsing preferences.
5. Save your settings.

Once enabled, Cowork will automatically use web browsing when your prompt requires information that is not available in your local files.

## Types of Research Tasks

Cowork's web research capability is well suited for a variety of tasks. Here are the most common categories:

### Market Research

Gather data about market size, target demographics, emerging trends, and industry benchmarks.

> "Research the current market size and growth projections for the online education industry in 2026."

### Pricing Research

Compare pricing across competitors, track price changes, and find the best rates for products or services.

> "Find and compare the monthly pricing plans for the top five email marketing platforms."

### News Monitoring

Stay up to date on industry news, product launches, regulatory changes, and public sentiment.

> "Summarize the most important AI industry news from the past two weeks."

### Lead Generation

Identify potential partners, clients, or vendors based on specific criteria.

> "Find 20 digital marketing agencies in Austin, Texas that specialize in B2B SaaS companies."

## Writing Effective Research Prompts

The quality of Cowork's research depends heavily on the clarity of your prompt. Follow these guidelines:

- **Be specific about the topic.** Instead of "research competitors," say "research the top 10 competitors in the project management software space."
- **State the desired output.** Tell Cowork what format you want — a list, a table, a spreadsheet, or a written summary.
- **Set boundaries.** Specify the geographic region, time frame, or industry segment if relevant.
- **Include evaluation criteria.** If you want a comparison, list the factors to compare (pricing, features, reviews, company size, etc.).

### Example Prompt

> "Research the top 10 competitors in the project management software industry and create a comparison spreadsheet. Include columns for company name, pricing tiers, key features, number of users, and customer rating."

## Limitations and Best Practices

### Limitations

- **Paywalled content** — Cowork cannot access content behind paywalls or login screens.
- **Real-time data** — Information may not reflect changes that occurred in the last few minutes.
- **Accuracy** — Web sources vary in reliability. Always verify critical data points from Cowork's research.
- **Rate limits** — Very large research tasks may need to be broken into smaller requests.

### Best Practices

- Start with a focused prompt and expand from there rather than asking for everything at once.
- Ask Cowork to cite its sources so you can verify key claims.
- Use follow-up prompts to drill into specific areas of interest.
- Combine web research with your own local data for richer analysis.
