## Introduction

Web research is only as useful as the output it produces. In this lesson, you will learn how to control the format, depth, and destination of Cowork's research reports. You will also learn how to combine web findings with your own local data and how to iterate on results to get exactly the insight you need.

---

## How Cowork Summarizes Findings

After Cowork completes a web research task, it compiles the raw information into a structured response. By default, Cowork will:

- Organize findings by topic or category.
- Highlight the most important data points.
- Remove redundant or low-quality information.
- Present results in a clear, readable format.

However, you have full control over how that summary looks. The key is telling Cowork what format you prefer.

---

## Customizing Report Format

Different situations call for different formats. Here are the most common options and how to request them.

### Bullet Points

Best for quick scanning and action items.

> "Summarize your research on email marketing platforms as a bulleted list. Group the bullets by platform name."

### Tables

Best for side-by-side comparisons.

> "Present your findings as a table with columns for Platform, Price, Key Features, Pros, and Cons."

### Executive Summary

Best for sharing with leadership or stakeholders who need the high-level picture.

> "Summarize your findings in a one-page executive brief with key takeaways. Start with a three-sentence overview, then list the top five insights, and end with a recommended next step."

### Detailed Report

Best for thorough documentation.

> "Write a detailed report on the state of AI-powered customer service tools. Include an introduction, methodology, findings by category, and a conclusion with recommendations."

## Saving Reports to Specific Folders

You can instruct Cowork to save its research output directly to a folder in your workspace.

### Example Prompts

> "Save the competitor analysis report to the /research/q1-2026/ folder as competitor-analysis.md."

> "Export the pricing comparison as a CSV file and place it in /data/pricing/."

This keeps your research organized and easy to find when you need to reference or update it later.

---

## Combining Web Research with Local Data

Some of the most powerful insights come from blending what Cowork finds on the web with data you already have. For example:

> "Compare the industry average customer acquisition cost you found online with the data in my marketing-spend.xlsx file. Highlight where we are above or below the benchmark."

> "I have a list of 50 leads in leads.csv. Research each company online and add columns for company size, industry, and recent news."

> "Cross-reference the competitor pricing you found with our internal pricing in pricing-strategy.xlsx. Identify where we are priced higher or lower."

By pointing Cowork to both web sources and local files, you get analysis that is grounded in your specific business context.

---

## Iterating on Research Results

Your first prompt rarely produces the perfect output. Cowork is designed for back-and-forth refinement. Use follow-up prompts to sharpen the results.

### Drill Deeper

> "Dig deeper into point number 3 — the trend around AI-powered onboarding. Find more data and examples."

### Change the Angle

> "Reframe this analysis from the perspective of a small business owner with fewer than 20 employees."

### Add More Detail

> "Expand the section on pricing. Include annual versus monthly billing differences and any available discounts."

### Simplify

> "This is too detailed. Condense the report into five key bullet points that I can share in a team meeting."

Each follow-up builds on the previous research, so Cowork does not need to start from scratch.

---

## Putting It All Together

Here is a complete workflow that combines everything from this module:

1. **Prompt:** "Research the top 10 CRM platforms for small businesses. Compare pricing, features, and user reviews."
2. **Follow-up:** "Present the results as a table and save it to /research/crm-comparison.csv."
3. **Follow-up:** "Now compare those findings with our current CRM costs in /data/tools-budget.xlsx."
4. **Follow-up:** "Summarize your findings in a one-page executive brief with key takeaways. Save it to /research/crm-executive-brief.md."
5. **Follow-up:** "Dig deeper into point number 3 — the migration costs. What are typical timelines and hidden fees?"

This iterative approach lets you start broad and progressively focus on what matters most.
